About USPS

Our Mission

The United States Postal Service (USPS) is an independent agency of the executive branch of the United States federal government responsible for providing postal service in the United States.

We are committed to providing trusted, affordable, universal service to all Americans, regardless of geography, at uniform price and quality. The Postal Service receives no tax dollars for operating expenses and relies on the sale of postage, products, and services to fund its operations.

Our History

Founded in 1775, the USPS has a rich history of connecting people and businesses across America. From the Pony Express to modern package delivery, we've evolved to meet the changing needs of our customers.

Our Network

With over 31,000 retail locations and 230,000 vehicles, we operate the largest civilian vehicle fleet in the world, delivering to every address in the nation six days a week.
